Project अपनापन

Project Apnapan is dedicated to fostering student agency and belonging in your school. Through our research, data-driven inquiry and evidence-based solutions, we aim to transform your school into inclusive environments where students feel empowered to take ownership of their learning and develop strong emotional connections with your school community.

Why we exist?

Think back to your school days!

Was there ever a moment when you felt truly VALUED, HEARD, SAFE, or CONNECTED?

Maybe it was a teacher who noticed your effort, a friend who supported you, or just knowing you had a place where you belonged.

Now, imagine the opposite—feeling INVISIBLE, UNHEARD, or UNSAFE. That's the difference between a student who thrives and one who disengages.

Where do you think your students are today? Are they connected with their teachers and feel belonging to your school community — or are they more isolated than ever?

The Problem

When students don't feel they belong, the impact isn't just emotional. It affects their learning, behavior, and well-being.

Academic Decline

When students feel disconnected, they engage less, lose motivation, and struggle academically. Research shows that a strong sense of belonging is linked to better classroom participation and higher achievement (Goodenow, 1993).

Behavioral Problems

Students without a sense of belonging are more likely to disengage, act out, or even stop attending school altogether. Research highlights a link between lack of belonging and increased absenteeism or disruptive behavior (Osterman, 2000).

Emotional Distress

Loneliness, anxiety, and even depression increase when students feel like they don't belong—especially during adolescence, a time when peer acceptance is crucial (Cause, 1986).

Lower Self-Esteem

Exclusion and isolation don't just affect academics—they shape a student's confidence and self-worth, impacting both their social and academic life.

What We Do

Our approach to creating an inclusive environment stands on four key pillars:

Action Research

We conduct action research in schools with students, teachers, and administrators to understand the current state of enabling factors such as student autonomy and a sense of belonging within the school community.

Collective Inquiry

We collaborate closely with schools, teachers, and educational leaders to design and evaluate interventions. This ensures that every solution is tailored to the unique context and needs of each school community.

Co-Design Solutions

We engage school communities in co-creating meaningful solutions based on the findings of our research, ensuring ownership and sustainability.
